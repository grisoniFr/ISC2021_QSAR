General references to regression methods

An excellent comprehensive reference to multivariate methods is The Elements of Statistical Learning: Data Mining, Inference, and Prediction (2009), by Trevor Hastie, Robert Tibshirani and Jerome Friedman (available on line here).

Details on Principal Component Regression (PCR) can be found here: I.E. Frank, J.H. Friedman (1993). "A Statistical View of Some Chemometrics Regression Tools". Technometrics. 35, 109 – 135 [link]

Details about theory and algorithms of Partial Least Squares (PLS) can be found here: M. Andersson, A comparison of nine PLS1 algorithms. Journal of Chemometrics 23 (2009) 518-529 [link]

There are many approaches based on the Genetic Algorithms (GAs) selection strategy. The approach implemented in this toolbox is inspired to that shown in the following paper: Leardi, R. and Lupianez, A. (1998). Genetic algorithms applied to feature selection in PLS regression: how and when to use them. Chemometrics and Intelligent Laboratory Systems, 41, 195-207 [link]

Details on Reshaped Sequential Replacement (RSR) an be found here: Cassotti, M., Grisoni, F., Todeschini, R. (2014). Reshaped Sequential Replacement: an efficient approach to variable selection. Chemometrics and Intelligent Laboratory Systems, 133, 136-148 [link] and Grisoni, F., Cassotti, M., Todeschini, R. (2014). Reshaped Sequential Replacement for variable selection in QSPR: comparison with other reference methods. Journal of Chemometrics, 28, 249-259 [link]

Calculation of regression measures related to external samples are described in this publication: V Consonni, D Ballabio, R Todeschini, Comments on the definition of the Q2 parameter for QSAR validation, Journal of chemical information and modeling 49 (7), 1669-1678 [link].
